Experience and Projects

Lyra

Founding Member & Communication Systems Technical Lead

I was one of the founding members of Lyra, a student organization formed to design and build a CubeSat as part of a NASA-supported student initiative. I served as the technical lead for the satellite’s communication subsystem, responsible for the design, development, and testing of a custom 433 MHz transceiver and integrated antenna solution.

The satellite was successfully launched in November 2025, marking Lyra’s first operational space mission.

Lyra Communication system

Technical Lead

For the project we designed the transceiver around a TI CC1200 radio chip, paired with an AVR microcontroller for configuration, control, and telemetry handling. The PCB pictured is out custum 433MHZ transceiver which was used as the transmitter in the satellite and as the ground station reciever. The result was a compact, reliable 433 MHz communication module in a CubeSat format.

FPAA dev board

The inital idea for my masters project was to use an Okika AN231 Field Programmable Analog Array to prototype a configurable front-end for Sonar systems. This pcb is a compact development board made for testing and simple system implementation of the FPAA.

The FPAA unfortunately didn't meet the requirements for my project, but it inspired the idea for my masters project

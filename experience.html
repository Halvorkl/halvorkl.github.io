Experience and Projects

Lyra

Founding Member & Communication Systems Technical Lead

I was one of the founding members of Lyra, a student organization formed to design and build a CubeSat as part of a NASA-supported student initiative. I served as the technical lead for the satellite’s communication subsystem, responsible for the design, development, and testing of a custom 433 MHz transceiver and integrated antenna solution.

The satellite was successfully launched in November 2025, marking Lyra’s first operational space mission.

Lyra Communication system

Technical Lead

For the project we designed the transceiver around a TI CC1200 radio chip, paired with an AVR microcontroller for configuration, control, and telemetry handling. The PCB pictured is out custum 433MHZ transceiver which was used as the transmitter in the satellite and as the ground station reciever. The result was a compact, reliable 433 MHz communication module in a CubeSat format.

L1 Opictal Lencing(LOL)

Co-author and payload engineer

LOL started as an exercise in conurrent engineering during the ESA CubeSat Summer School course, but has evolved further into a paper presented at IAC and published in Astra aeronautica. We are currently also applying for an ESA stipend to realise the project.

From the abstract: "The terrascope concept uses Earth’s atmosphere as a lens to amplify light from distant astro- nomical bodies along a focal line extending to Lagrange 1. If successfully implemented, a terrascope could revolutionise deep space observation by enabling large-scale and high-resolution exoplanet spectroscopy and, potentially, the discovery of new exoplanets. On these premises, the ’L1 Optical Lensing (LOL)’ mission aims to realise the amplification and resolution of the terrascope concept by using a satellite placed at a Hill radius distance from Earth, serving as a scientific demonstrator."
